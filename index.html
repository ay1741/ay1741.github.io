Some of the tasks that I completed were analyze & completed Visio Drawings for 125 global applications in multiple environments, plan and execute the shipping of server racks across the country, present data center simplification progress to CTO monthly, create exception workflow for Schenectady based applications and help analyze ways to combine maximo instances within the business.

Working at GE was such an incredible experience since I got to be part of the time while GE was transforming for the Alstom integration adding 50,000 employees to the already 300,000 employee base. I toured the manufacturing plant on campus that manufactures and repairs steam turbines and also was able to visit GE's Global Reseach Center that has the task of developing technology that you will see in 5-10 years, the research center is one of the largest private research centers in the world. Once a week all ITLP interns sat down with a CIO among the business talking about being successful as a leader and helping the business succeed. Finally, I was part of a commitee to research technologies the business could use to help the field technicians be more efficient focusing on wearable teachnology.

RIT - Electric Vehicle Team

Electrical Team Engineer & Project Manager | 2014 - Present | 15+ hours per week

Joining the Electrical Vechicle Team at RIT has been a huge step for me in growing as an engineer. This is the next level of FIRST robotics, were there are no build rules and very little regulations. We can sit on a project for a month, a year or not at all. Of course, we strive to push out projects as fast as we can while we continue to develop high quality products. I spend all my free time here after I complete my school work; I spend about 15-20 hours per week if not more on current and future projects, I act as a project manager and key electrical engineer for the team.

Our newest project is the development of a superbike(pictured above) to race 1-2 times a year against other electric motorcycles. We just completed and raced REV1 in July 2015 based off of a 2005 Kawasaki Ninja ZX6RR frame. We developed and manufactured a custom battery box(pictured top left) that includes a custom battery management system powering a Zero Motors Z-Force 75-7 controlled by a Sevcon size 6 Motor Controller with a custom program profile. Some future features include live data reporting/ analytics, active balancing and more power!

You can read more about the team on our website here.

You can see more photos of the projects I helped the team complete on my Coroflot.
